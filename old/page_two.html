Transportation

In 1908, the Model T car is introduced, and Henry Ford develops the "Five Dollar Work Day", enabling workers to afford the cars they are assembling. World War I halts much of the initial momentum towards the car as efforts are put towards the war. But car ownership takes off soon after the war, and motorized vehicles become beneficial in many ways such as farming.

By 1914, planes have been created, but they are rudimentary. Due to advances in technology brought on by World War I, by 1937 passenger planes have arrived. It takes 17 hours to travel from Newark to Los Angeles.

Rail is also advancing. By the 1930's, it takes 3 days to get from New York to Los Angeles.

The Standard Time is officially made law by the Standard Time Act in 1918.

Communication

In 1915 the first trans-continental phone is developed enabling calls between different continents.

In the 1930's, radio is a requirement on ships as a safety measure.

Scientific Theory

1905 is 'The Year of Miracles'. Albert Einstein furthers scientific theory, and becomes a super star for his work on special and general relativity. It becomes known space and time bends around masses. Time is relative to the observer.

Photography and Video

Portable and affordable cameras such as the Brownie are starting to be developed.

By 1928 the 16mm Kodascope was created for capturing video.
