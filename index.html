My Journey of Learning the Uke


I've always wanted to learn the ukulele. Recently, I was visiting my friend, Jennie Tulenchik. She is an AWESOME musician, and she plays the instrument, so I very politely asked her if I could borrow one of her ukes since she has a couple, and she said yes!

We spent an evening together. She played me some of her music, and explained some things to me. I thought the ukulele was a simple instrument, but I soon realized it is as complicated as any. For instance, there are four major types of ukes:

SOPRANO
 This is the smallest of them, and it makes the sound most commonly associated with a uke.
CONCERT
 Just slightly bigger than the soprano, so it has a slightly bigger sound.
TENOR
 A bit bigger...
BARITONE
 The grand daddy. The biggest of them all. These are tuned differently.

That's the major difference between them all. They just get bigger. I was given a soprano style. After some research, I found it to be the most common. That was helpful as I learned, because most of the online instructional materials were using this type of ukulele.

So on we go! I'm going to show you what I've learned!
